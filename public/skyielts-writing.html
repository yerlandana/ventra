Writing band

5.0Modest user
CEFR B1

You can write a response that is on topic and the reader gets your general meaning, but ideas are underdeveloped or repeated, paragraphing is unclear, and you rely on simple sentences with frequent errors. Linking words are basic or mechanical ('Firstly, Secondly'), and word choice is limited. The essay often runs under length or doesn't fully address every part of the task.

Do this first — fastest way to 5.5

Develop ONE idea per body paragraph: state it, explain why, give one specific example. Depth beats a long list of shallow points.

Writing band

5.5Modest+ user
CEFR B1+

Your essays are recognisably structured and address the task, and the reader rarely gets lost. But your ideas are still developed only briefly, your range of grammar and vocabulary is narrow, and errors — though not blocking meaning — are frequent enough to be noticeable in almost every sentence. You're doing the right things; you're just not yet doing them with enough range or control.

Do this first — fastest way to 6.0

Pick fewer ideas and go deeper. Two well-explained reasons beat five mentioned ones — and depth is exactly what separates 5.5 from 6.0.

The jumpWhat turns a 5.0 into a 5.5

Topic · Do the advantages of city living outweigh the disadvantages?

Band 5.0

“City has many advantages. There are many jobs and good hospitals. But there is also pollution and it is expensive. So there are advantages and disadvantages.”

Band 5.5

“City living offers clear practical advantages, the most important being opportunity. Cities concentrate employers, universities and hospitals in one place, which means a resident can change jobs or access specialist care without relocating. Although this convenience comes at the cost of higher rents and pollution, for most working people the access to opportunity outweighs these drawbacks.”

What changed ↑

  • Commits to a position ('outweighs') instead of sitting on the fence
  • Develops the advantage with a mechanism ('concentrate… which means…')
  • Uses concession ('Although…') to weigh both sides, not just list them

Writing band

6.0Competent user
CEFR B2

You address all parts of the task with a clear position, organise information logically, and the reader follows you with no real effort. You use a mix of sentence types and an adequate vocabulary. What holds you at 6 is consistency: ideas can still be over-generalised, cohesion is sometimes mechanical, and although you produce complex sentences, errors in them are still frequent and your range of vocabulary stays 'safe'. This is the band most undergraduate courses ask for.

Do this first — fastest way to 6.5

Make your support specific and accurate. Trade one 'safe' generalisation for a concrete example, and proofread your complex sentences — accurate complexity is the heart of the jump to 7.

The jumpWhat turns a 5.5 into a 6.0

Topic · Some say governments should tax unhealthy food. To what extent do you agree?

Band 5.5

“I agree that governments should tax unhealthy food because it is bad for health. If food is expensive, people will not buy it. This will make people more healthy and reduce diseases.”

Band 6.0

“I largely agree that taxing unhealthy food is justified, primarily as a tool to shift behaviour. When the price of sugary drinks rises, consumption tends to fall, particularly among younger buyers who are most price-sensitive. Mexico's 2014 soda tax, for instance, led to a measurable drop in sales, which suggests that fiscal pressure can nudge diets in a healthier direction without an outright ban.”

What changed ↑

  • Adds a real-world example (Mexico) instead of a circular claim
  • 'price-sensitive', 'fiscal pressure', 'nudge' — precise, academic range
  • 'which suggests that…' develops the consequence rather than asserting it

The jumpWhat turns a 6.0 into a 6.5

Topic · Many believe technology has made people less social. Discuss.

Band 6.0

“Technology has made people less social in some ways. People spend a lot of time on their phones and they don't talk to each other face to face. However, technology also helps people stay connected with friends who live far away.”

Band 6.5

“The claim that technology erodes social life is only half the picture. It is true that constant phone use can crowd out face-to-face interaction, particularly at the dinner table. Yet the same tools sustain relationships that distance would otherwise dissolve — a grandparent video-calling grandchildren abroad is more connected, not less. The real shift, then, is not less socialising but a change in its form.”

What changed ↑

  • Reframes instead of listing 'for' and 'against' — shows synthesis
  • 'crowd out', 'dissolve', 'erodes' — flexible, precise lexis
  • Closes with an analytical conclusion ('not less… but a change in its form')

The jumpWhat turns a 6.5 into a 7.0

Topic · Should higher education be free for everyone?

Band 6.5

“There is a compelling case for free higher education, mainly because it widens access. When tuition is free, students from poor families can attend university, which reduces inequality. However, it is expensive for governments, so taxes may need to rise.”

Band 7.0

“There is a compelling case for free higher education, though it rests on a trade-off that is easy to overlook. Removing tuition undeniably widens access: talented students from low-income households no longer self-select out of university for fear of debt. What is often overlooked, however, is who ultimately pays — funded through general taxation, 'free' degrees can transfer wealth from non-graduates to a largely middle-class student body. The fairer model, therefore, may be not abolition of fees but a repayment system tied to later income.”

What changed ↑

  • Goes beyond pro/con to a genuine analytical insight (who pays)
  • 'self-select out', 'transfer wealth', 'tied to later income' — precise, low-frequency
  • Conclusion proposes a synthesised position, not a restatement
  • Error-free complex sentences with embedded clauses
