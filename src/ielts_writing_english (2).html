Part 1 — Section 01
Understanding pie charts
A pie chart shows proportional data — how a whole is divided into parts. Your job is NOT to list every number. Identify the dominant segment, the smallest, group similar-sized slices, and compare them to each other.

Example: How people in the UK spend leisure time (2022)

TV & Streaming34%
Social Media22%
Outdoor Activities18%
Reading14%
Gaming12%

🥇 Dominant segment

Find the largest slice first — this goes in your overview. It's your most important data point.

🔻 Smallest segment

Always contrast it with the largest. That gap is usually the most striking feature to highlight.

⚖️ Similar-sized groups

Look for slices close in size and group them: "Three categories accounted for roughly equal proportions…"

📊 Two charts: compare

Identify what changed most and what stayed similar. The overview must cover both charts.

✓ ALWAYS DO THIS
Describe the biggest and smallest in your overview
Group similar-sized segments together
Compare segments ("twice as large as")
Paraphrase the chart title in your intro
Write at least 150 words
✗ NEVER DO THIS
List every percentage without comparing
Copy the chart title word-for-word
Give your opinion or explain reasons
Write a conclusion paragraph
Forget to include units (%)
Part 1 — Section 02
Essay structure — step by step
1

Introduction — paraphrase the task (2–3 sentences)

Restate what the chart shows using different words. Do NOT copy the title. Mention context: year, country, topic.

"The pie chart illustrates the proportion of…" / "The diagram provides a breakdown of…"
2

Overview — 2 most striking features (2–3 sentences) ★ Most important

Identify the largest and smallest segment plus one other pattern. No specific figures here — just the big picture.

"Overall, it is clear that… accounted for the largest share, while… represented the smallest proportion."
3

Body paragraph 1 — top segments with figures (3–4 sentences)

Describe the 2–3 largest segments with exact percentages. Use comparison language ("nearly a third," "more than double").

"TV and streaming made up the largest segment at 34%, followed by social media at 22%."
4

Body paragraph 2 — smaller segments with comparisons (3–4 sentences)

Cover remaining segments. Group similar ones together. For two charts, compare the changes here.

"The remaining three categories were more evenly distributed, ranging from 12% to 18%."

Fill-in essay template — single pie chart

150–185 words
Introduction

The pie chart [illustrates / presents / shows] the [distribution / breakdown / proportion] of [topic] in [country/context] in [year]. The data is divided into [number] categories.

Overview — most important paragraph

Overall, it is evident that [largest category] accounted for the greatest share, while [smallest category] represented the smallest proportion. [Add a second key feature].

Body paragraph 1 — main segments

[Largest] made up the largest segment at [%], which was [nearly / more than / roughly] [fraction] of the total. This was followed by [2nd] at [%], and [3rd] at [%].

Body paragraph 2 — remaining segments

The remaining categories were more modest. [Category] and [category] accounted for [%] and [%] respectively, while [smallest] represented only [%] — the smallest share of all.

Sample Band 7+ answer — leisure time pie chart

~160 words
Introduction

The pie chart illustrates the proportion of time that people in the UK devoted to various leisure activities in 2022, divided into five categories.

Overview ★

Overall, watching television and streaming dominated leisure time, while gaming represented the smallest share. The remaining three categories occupied a combined share of just under half.

Body 1

TV and streaming accounted for the largest proportion at 34% — more than one third of total leisure time. Social media was second at 22%, followed by outdoor pursuits at 18%.

Body 2

Reading and gaming represented the two smallest categories at 14% and 12% respectively. Gaming was marginally the least popular activity, accounting for just over one tenth of all leisure time.

Part 1 — Section 03
Hybrid task — pie chart + graph
A hybrid task gives you TWO different chart types — most commonly a pie chart with a line graph, bar chart, or table. The key challenge is connecting both. Show how they relate — never write two completely independent descriptions.

Golden rule: Both charts must appear in your overview. Your body paragraphs either cover each chart separately OR group related information across both thematically. Never treat them as two essays.

Option A: One paragraph per chart (safer)

160–185 words
Introduction

The [pie chart] and [line graph / bar chart / table] provide information about [overall topic] in [context/year].

Overview — features from BOTH charts

Overall, [key finding from pie chart], while the [second chart] reveals that [key trend]. Together, the charts suggest that [broader connection].

Body 1 — pie chart details

[Largest segment] accounted for the greatest proportion at [%], followed by [second] at [%], while [smallest] made up only [%].

Body 2 — graph details + link

The [graph] shows that [main trend]. [Key data points]. This complements the pie chart, as [explain the connection].

Option B: Thematic paragraphs — higher band potential

170–200 words
Introduction

The charts illustrate [topic]. The pie chart shows [what], while the [graph type] tracks [what] over time.

Overview

Overall, [dominant theme across both charts] emerges as the most significant finding, with [secondary theme] also notable.

Body 1 — theme 1 drawing from both charts

In terms of [theme], the pie chart shows that [data], and this is reflected in the [graph], which demonstrates that [supporting trend].

Body 2 — theme 2 drawing from both charts

Conversely, [smaller categories] represented a minor share at [%]. The [graph] similarly indicates that [low/declining trend].

Sample Band 7 hybrid answer

Task: The pie chart shows the percentage of household income spent in different categories in the UK in 2020. The line graph shows how spending on housing and food changed between 2010 and 2020.

Sample — household spending (pie + line graph)

~185 words
Introduction

The pie chart and line graph provide complementary information about household expenditure in the UK. While the pie chart shows the distribution of spending across categories in 2020, the line graph illustrates how two specific areas evolved over the preceding decade.

Overview ★

Housing was by far the dominant expenditure category in 2020, and the line graph confirms this dominance grew steadily throughout the decade. Food and transport represented the next largest shares, while leisure remained comparatively low.

Body 1 — pie chart

According to the pie chart, housing accounted for the largest share at 38%, nearly double the proportion spent on food (20%). Transport and clothing made up 15% and 12% respectively, while leisure represented the smallest segment at just 8%.

Body 2 — line graph + link

The line graph reveals housing costs rose consistently from 28% in 2010 to 38% by 2020. Food spending remained stable throughout, fluctuating narrowly between 18% and 21%. This supports the pie chart's finding that housing is the most financially burdensome category for UK households.

Part 2 — Section 05
Part 2: People's interaction essay
Questions about how people interact — impact of technology, social isolation, community bonds, online vs. face-to-face relationships — are among the most common Part 2 topics. This section covers Discussion and Opinion essay types.

Discussion — both sides

"Discuss both views and give your own opinion." Cover both sides AND state your position.

Opinion — agree/disagree

"To what extent do you agree?" Commit to one position and develop strong arguments for it.

Problem + solution

"What are the causes? What solutions?" Two-part structure — cover both equally.

Advantages + disadvantages

"Discuss the advantages and disadvantages." Cover both, then optionally give your view on balance.

4-paragraph essay structure
1

Introduction (~50 words)

Paraphrase the topic. Give a background sentence. State your position clearly — avoid "In this essay I will…"

"While some argue that [view A], others contend that [view B]. This essay examines both before concluding that [your position]."
2

Body paragraph 1 — first view / argument (~100 words)

Topic sentence → Explanation → Example/evidence → Mini-conclusion linking back. ONE clear idea per paragraph.

"On the one hand, [view]. This is because [reason]. For instance, [example]. As a result, [consequence]."
3

Body paragraph 2 — second view / stronger argument (~100 words)

Discussion: present the other side. Opinion: your strongest argument. Open with a contrast marker.

"On the other hand, [view]. [Explanation]. [Example]. Therefore, [mini-conclusion]."
4

Conclusion (~50 words)

Restate (don't copy) your main points. Give your clear position. Must be 100% consistent with your introduction.

"In conclusion, although [opposing view], I firmly believe that [your position] because [brief reason]."
PEEL in practice — worked example
Paragraph: "Technology reduces face-to-face interaction"
Point
Technology has undeniably reduced the frequency of face-to-face social interaction in modern societies.
Explain
This is because digital platforms allow people to maintain relationships remotely, reducing the perceived need to meet in person. Younger generations now prefer the convenience of online communication.
Example
For instance, studies have shown the average person sends over 100 text messages per day, yet meets friends face-to-face only twice a week — a significant shift from pre-smartphone behaviour.
Link back
Consequently, while technology facilitates constant communication, it may simultaneously be eroding the depth of human relationships that can only fully develop through in-person interaction.

Full sample Band 7+ essay

Question: "Some people believe that modern technology is making it harder for people to have meaningful relationships with others. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

Sample — technology & meaningful relationships

~265 words · Band 7
Introduction

There is growing concern that the rise of digital technology is fundamentally altering the nature of human relationships, making them shallower and less emotionally fulfilling. While technology undeniably offers new ways to connect, I largely agree that it presents significant challenges to the development of truly meaningful bonds.

Body 1 — main argument

The primary reason is that digital communication lacks the non-verbal cues essential to deep human connection — eye contact, tone of voice, and body language all play a critical role. Research in social psychology suggests over 70% of emotional communication is non-verbal, meaning digital channels convey less than a third of what a face-to-face conversation does. As a result, online relationships tend to remain superficial and transactional.

Body 2 — concession + counter

Supporters might argue that video calling and social media enable people to maintain long-distance relationships that would otherwise be impossible — a valid point. However, this convenience can encourage people to substitute screen-based contact for in-person meetings even when the latter is possible. Over time, the habit of choosing digital convenience may erode the interpersonal skills needed to sustain meaningful bonds.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while technology has expanded the reach of human communication, I firmly believe it poses a genuine threat to the quality of our relationships. Individuals and communities should prioritise face-to-face interaction and ensure digital tools remain supplementary rather than primary channels of human connection.

What examiners look for

Task Achievement

  • Fully answers all parts
  • Clear consistent position
  • Well-developed ideas with examples
  • Part 1: overview + accurate data

Coherence & Cohesion

  • Logical paragraph sequence
  • Clear topic sentences
  • Varied cohesive devices
  • Each paragraph has ONE idea

Lexical Resource

  • Topic-specific vocabulary
  • Paraphrasing, not repeating
  • Less common vocabulary used accurately
  • Rare spelling errors

Grammar Range & Accuracy

  • Mix of simple and complex sentences
  • Conditionals, passives, relative clauses
  • Accurate tenses and punctuation
  • Errors don't impede meaning
